# Glass Coralers


- **Capital**: Inkland Hold (10,100)
- **Leader**: Admiral Edward (Lv9)
  - Coastal cities grow & recover soldiers at 5× the normal investment rate — you are buffeted by Coraler refugees fleeing the Inner Sea.
  - Your soldiers are entirely Coraler. You have the only functional naval stealth.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Secure a permanent claim on Glass for the Coralers. Expel the Venetians from Glass as revenge & for future protection — your interests lie in their *complete* genocide.

**It's not just Glass that's burning.** Plague of the reef, economic strangulation, & civil war has swept the Inner Sea. Sixteen hundred years of *total* domination over the waves where no man nor bird nor beast could touch you for fear of watery death had lulled you & your fellow Coralers into a sense of false security that was obliterated with one act of a foreign god.

Now, with vast white concrete shells hiding amphibious cities & stealth legions that could sweep the economic centre of the Grand Disc hollowed out by deathly plague & subsequent war, you're on the run, desperate, & struggling to find a new home. And on Glass's crumbling shores, that home was built through bloody conquest.

**You have no plan B. This is not your land, but it will be your land, because you will die otherwise.** Nobody has ever challenged the Coralers until five years ago. Now is the time for stability, that revenge may follow. Humans know nothing & will *learn* nothing that the depths have to teach. On a world with 40-mile diameter visibility on the open sea, *your every soldier is a submarine*. The brush of a Coraler's tentacled fist over a contract ended empires. One *word* summoned blockades that crushed trade Disc-wide. And a bunch of *arrogant upstarts* think a *single plague* will put your people down?

You are the most accomplished leader the Coraler clans have ever known. Every new wave of carnage brings you reinforcements — desperate, furious warriors with a world to reclaim. **See to it the merchants & empires of Man learns what it means to fear the sea.**
