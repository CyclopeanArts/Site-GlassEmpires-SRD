Aia's Moon is a strange & unpleasant landscape for anything not born there.

**Half the Moon is blanketed in a nearly perpetual night**, as the Moon is situated further from Aia than the Sun is, and therefore never sees light on the side facing away from Aia. Even if you're not on the dark side of the Moon, **the entire Moon has a nearly-constant cloud cover that rarely dissipates**, granting only very brief glimpses of direct sunlight.

**There is an extremely powerful & omnipresent curvature visible in all directions**, because the Moon is only ~300 miles in diameter. This creates a "claustrophobic horizon" effect. **You can only see around half a mile into the distance on flat terrain**, around *40 times* shorter than on Aia's surface (where on a clear day, one can see for maybe 20 miles). Everywhere you stand, it always feels like you're standing on a very gentle hill, even if the ground is level. For similar reasons, **flat maps are almost totally useless beyond an extremely local scale**, because distortions grow so rapidly.

**The entire Moon essentially shares a single weather system**. When it rains on the Moon, it rains *everywhere*. The ~280,000 square miles of terrain pegs the Moon at around the size of Texas, or France plus the U.K. combined.

**There is no consistent method of keeping time**. Without the Sun orbiting in a regular manner & with the omnipresent cloud cover, the entire surface is perpetually dark, with only occasional breaks in the sky on the bright side of the moon. Any reference to "days" is purely for convenience's sake.

And that's not even getting into the geography, flora, or fauna.

## Getting to the Moon

- **Kill a Moon Rabbit Operator, steal its Rabbit Guard's Feet Feature with alchemy, and then jump to the Moon**. This is extremely risky & is usually a one-way trip because the Moon is under perpetual cloud cover. Once you pass through the clouds, Aia isn't visible until you can climb to the top of a Lunarian crystal spire or one of the mountains that penetrates the cloud cover, which means you can't return *even if* you brought a spare to return with.
- **Find a Lunarian Wormhole & pass through it, and then escape detection & expulsion by the Lunarian Royal Guard**. This is extremely risky because the Lunarians keep extremely tight watch over every wormhole they operate specifically to prevent Aian creatures from slipping through.
- **Build a magical contraption & fly to the Moon**. Not impossible, but very unlikely & difficult. I don't really know what kind of engineering would be required to achieve this. It's certainly beyond any mortal ability.
- **Magic**. A sufficiently powerful spirit of movement could likely teleport you to the Moon in a manner not dissimilar to the moon rabbit operators.

## Leaving the Moon

- **Tell the Lunarians you're there**. If the Lunarian Royal Family learns that an Aian made it to the Moon, they will happily strip you of everything from the Moon you possess & kick you butt-naked out of one of their wormholes to freeze to death in a distant wilderness. It's not a dignified or easy method of exit, but they *will* let you go.
- **Kill a Moon Rabbit Operator, steal its lunar hop & climb a crystal spire**. The Lunarian towers that penetrate the cloud cover are the launching points for rabbit operators that get deployed to Aia. During the lunar apex, anyone that can match its hop can reach any point on Aia.

## Lunarian Kingdom

*Ludicrously* elitist towards anyone on the surface world. Absolutely not better than them in any way, except that they actually are. Lunarians are beautiful, intelligent, highly developed, mostly ignorant to the world beyond themselves, & kept in a developmental stasis by their rulers, who have no desire to *ever* allow the Moon to change in any meaningful way.

### Lunarian Royal Family

The Lunarian Royal Family consists of the Lunarian Princess & her direct family. They are all immortal, *terrifyingly* deadly, & essentially content to stay in their palace.

They have layers & layers of reactive defenses of every sort that can be imagined & many of those that cannot. They have Continuity of Governance protocols that extend back to the creation of the Moon. They have plans for every possible contingency. They are hell-bent on things staying *exactly the way they are* within their bubble and will brook no competition nor challenge that will disrupt them in any way.

And yet, they have weaknesses.

- **Passivity**: Most of their defenses are based on passive detection. They make no personal effort to protect themselves.
- **Aian Ignorance**: They cannot and do not pay attention to most of the Grand Disc. They assume that no meaningful force can or would fly from Aia to the Disc without Lunarian magic/wormholes.
	- They watch wormhole usage closely for intruders, but have no tracking for celestial object entry & exits, in part because birds & some other creatures regularly visit the Moon.
- **Novel Magic**: They usually rely on broad-spectrum anti-magic effects rather than specific anti-magic effects in part because of the difficulty of predicting magical evolution.

Despite this, their ability to deploy deadly troops (albeit somewhat incompetent ones, & in small numbers) to anywhere on Aia instantaneously combined with their enormous magical & technological advantages means that they could freely manipulate the state of Aia on a whim if they chose to do so. (Some say that the Lunarian Princess drove Kain's wife away in order to drive him from the throne of Glass.)

## Practical role in a game

The Lunarians have a stockpile of enormously powerful & dangerous magical weaponry & utilities that could turn the Grand Disc on its head if even a handful were brought back to the surface. Going to the Moon & coming back is something that Lv10+ PCs would do in order to supercharge their conquests with world-wrecking power at the risk of bringing down the wrath of aeons-old proto-gods onto their heads.

Alternatively, much of the Moon is not actually under Lunarian occupation as it consists of a bizarre & deadly alien landscape with a variety of interesting rewards that by themselves could be extraordinarily valuable even if they didn't change the fate of empires (merely kingdoms).
