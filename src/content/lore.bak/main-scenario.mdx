## Victory Conditions

**The main goal of every major Faction is to conquer Glass**. In order to conquer Glass, *one* of the two following states must be achieved:

- **De Jure Emperor**: Meet all three of the following conditions:
  - Control the City of Leaves.
  - Be declared Emperor with the blessings of Kain *or* Prince Sūn.
  - Control at least 50% of the island's area, as measured by the largest contiguous area that can be drawn between your Settlements & the ocean containing no Settlement not under your control.
- **Conquest**: Annihilate or subjugate every other major faction.

If either state is met, the Faction who has done so is *victorious*. This is the intended & most probably "clean" end state, but of course, this being a role-playing game, it may yet go on.

## Bonus Victories

Each major Ruler also has an objective of their own. If a Ruler achieves their own objective by the end of the game, they are *also* victorious, even if they are not the new Emperor.

## Major Factions

### House Eagle

- **Capital**: Warnwater (12,000)
  - Winding underground *Jade Catacombs* form intricate network beneath the city
- **Leader**: Lady Eagle (Lv9)
  - Venetian League starts allied with you & will loan you unlimited sums so long as you still appear to be potentially capable of winning the war.
  - You may use the House Eagle army culture instead of the Glassian Human set.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Secure exclusive banking rights for the Venetians over all of Glass. You will be rewarded handsomely.

"Protected" from the civil war by virtue of your trade with the **Venetian League's merchants**, you are a **cosmopolitan opportunistic warmonger** bathed in banking influence from a young age. You believe that working alongside the Venetians will allow you & your House to seize total control over the island of Glass — and even if that means you'd be a colonial or puppet ruler, you're fine with that. The Venetians' investment is riding on your success.

You are a psychopathic hedonist. **Your primary interest is in getting filthy rich** & enjoying everything Aia has to offer, which is a tremendous deal for a wealthy lord.

### House Arcades

- **Capital**: Arcadia (8,000)
  - Hilltop *Snow Jesters' Temple* provides burial & reanimation services
  - The *Panopticon* grants instantaneous vision across the continent sharp enough to make out individual people provided the operator's attention. (*Practically, you know all orders & movements as soon as they happen & can watch for specific events in specific places.*)
  - 32 loyal Jiangshi (6,440 fearless BR w/ full Retainers, \$4,800/month)
    - Every month, you may replace 1, up to 32.
- **Leader**: Lord Arcades (Lv8)
  - The commoners distrust you.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Expel *all* foreigners from Glass (the coralers, Venetians, Hydreans, etc.) or return them to the servant caste where they belong (the salamanders, harpies) — either alive or (un)dead.

**You are the son of a line of fore-fathers going back to the earliest age of Glassian history**, and this war has gone on long enough. It's an embarrassment to your blood. An initial effort to revive the ancient imperial infrastructure has seen success, and so you now seek its end through the judicious application of **your unparalleled black magic** combined with the power of the **Panopticon**.

**You are a necromantic isolationist.** Total control over Glass is the only way to drive out the invading forces — and your only hope of posing enough of a threat to **drive out the demonic creditors you've borrowed from** in order to fuel your ambitions. You are sworn to the island's people, gods & history, but have contempt for the immortal Kain as a failure & a coward who ran from power in a moment of weakness. You will be better. Man cannot shy from horrors when they're what is needed. **There is only one grip strong enough to reforge this empire, and it is yours.**

### House Beacon

- **Capital**: Sun Bay (5,770)
- **Leader**: Lord Beacon (Lv8)
  - The Hydreans have shipped you Jesuits & Zealots.
  - You possess 3 of the 5 metals mines on Glass & harbor an uneasy relationship with the salamander operators.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Convert all remaining major factions to Chimatocoatl's cult & sacrifice all of the Gods of Glass upon her altar.

You are the heir of a Lord who lost his original lands & home to a rampaging native god, and while your father may have forgiven it, you have not. The Hydrean Jesuits discovered this, and, in secret, reached out to you with condolences & an opportunity for revenge. **You are a whole-hearted believer in the Hydrean doctrine**. The false gods are the broken fragments of Chimatocoatl, and they must be captured & reunited with her.

**You are still a thoroughbred Glassian.** You believe in the strength of Glass & the superiority of its people over the rest of the Disc. You may or may not respect Kain for what he is, but you know he isn't sufficient as-is for the state of the island. **His cult has failed.** The gods are killing everyone. **The Rainbow Serpent of Wisdom is the *only* thing that can bring a permanent peace to the island, and you are her sword.**

### Commons Revolt

- **Capital**: Cerces (8,000)
- **Leader**: General Ram (Lv8)
  - One of the five salamander mining guilds have an uneasy alliance with you in the belief you will liberate them.
  - If General Ram is leading a siege, the Families of a Settlement *cannot* be added to the BR of the defenders while determining siege duration.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Stop the war by any means and totally strip the Governors (Lords & Magistrates) of their special privileges. Establish an at least nominally republican government *or* persuade Kain to return to the throne as God-Emperor of Glass.

**Everything went to hell the instant Kain abdicated his throne.** There was five years of "peace" between the *Autumn Massacre* of the Imperial court and the outbreak of the Coraler invasion, & even those were fraught with confusion, accusations of illegitimacy, murder, rapid imposition of tyranny, defection & decay. And before anyone could finish handling the invasion, the Mother Empress went insane & *everyone started killing each other* for the right to rule. All of the old wonders are broken. Famines are raging. Bandits flood the lands. **The entire world is on fire. If someone doesn't act, there will be nothing left.**

**You are the populist military leader of a massive Commons revolt** against the corruption, decadence, and collective failures of the entire leadership of Glass that has been steadily accumulating since the day the Second Phoenix delegated his command to an unworthy mortal bastard. **Born to a potter & raised by a Magistrate as the "son" of a Lord**, you've seen every level of Glassian society & every flaw & crack that's splintered this society from cornice to pedestal. Every single one of these "Lords" is an illegitimate pretender. **Kill them all**.

Your plan is simple: Force every single Lord to bend the knee & renounce their nobility, march into the temple-city of Heaven's Peak, and either bring Kain back to the throne as God-Emperor or set up a republican government with his counsel.

You are not strictly egalitarian & are still knowing of the superiority of Glassians, but your popular base includes many salamanders, harpies, and a few Coralers who believe that *at the very least* you won't throw them out of Glass.

- **Principal Assets**: You are the most accomplished & dangerous general in present Glassian history. You are beloved across the Commons to almost the extent that Kain is, and receive no resistance from the average man. You have minimal experience in stewardship. You are a murderer and a mercenary. Every ounce of populist energy you summon is the embodiment of rage, and if it has form it is only because you shape it. Your people are furiously loyal but unstable and untrained. Eschewing the extant elite means you have *no counter-elite to work with*, which is the most dangerous possible position for an upstart rebellion.

### Glass Coralers

- **Capital**: Inkland Hold (10,100)
- **Leader**: Admiral Edward (Lv9)
  - Coastal cities grow & recover soldiers at 5× the normal investment rate — you are buffeted by Coraler refugees fleeing the Inner Sea.
  - Your soldiers are entirely Coraler. You have the only functional naval stealth.
- **Bonus Victory Agenda**: Secure a permanent claim on Glass for the Coralers. Expel the Venetians from Glass as revenge & for future protection — your interests lie in their *complete* genocide.

**It's not just Glass that's burning.** Plague of the reef, economic strangulation, & civil war has swept the Inner Sea. Sixteen hundred years of *total* domination over the waves where no man nor bird nor beast could touch you for fear of watery death had lulled you & your fellow Coralers into a sense of false security that was obliterated with one act of a foreign god.

Now, with vast white concrete shells hiding amphibious cities & stealth legions that could sweep the economic centre of the Grand Disc hollowed out by deathly plague & subsequent war, you're on the run, desperate, & struggling to find a new home. And on Glass's crumbling shores, that home was built through bloody conquest.

**You have no plan B. This is not your land, but it will be your land, because you will die otherwise.** Nobody has ever challenged the Coralers until five years ago. Now is the time for stability, that revenge may follow. Humans know nothing & will *learn* nothing that the depths have to teach. On a world with 40-mile diameter visibility on the open sea, *your every soldier is a submarine*. The brush of a Coraler's tentacled fist over a contract ended empires. One *word* summoned blockades that crushed trade Disc-wide. And a bunch of *arrogant upstarts* think a *single plague* will put your people down?

You are the most accomplished leader the Coraler clans have ever known. Every new wave of carnage brings you reinforcements — desperate, furious warriors with a world to reclaim. **See to it the merchants & empires of Man learns what it means to fear the sea.**
