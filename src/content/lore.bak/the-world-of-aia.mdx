**Aia** is a **spinning flat disc** approximating **2,000 miles in diameter** ringed by a **tremendous swirling blizzard** upon its rim which sits atop a vast ocean of heavy liquid called **the Blackwater**, of such density that even lead will float. Beyond this is the astronomers' hypothesized **Telluric Veil**, which separates the heavens from the Blackwater.

Aia is *obviously* flat because **there is no curvature**; on a flat ocean, one can see for nearly ~20 miles before atmospheric scattering blinds you.

## The Sun

**Aia's Sun does not consistently rise in the east & set in the west**. Instead, it appears to change orbits on a daily basis, because Aia (*or possibly the external cosmos*) is *spinning*. Because the sun's position changes on a daily basis, **sundials do not work on Aia** unless rotated to match the sun's new location at the start of every day.

The Sun is not *known* to be round, but is *believed* to be so. It is believed to be around the same size as the Moon & *obviously* orbits closer to Aia than the Moon as it occasionally passes between the Moon & Aia (a "solar eclipse").

## The Moon

**There is a kingdom on the Moon & it hates you**. It will *never* extend its fabulous & wonderful offerings, except to those kings who can provide what the Moon lacks. Through the dense white swirling clouds, you can occasionally glimpse their crystalline spires — and *only ever* will you glimpse it.

**The Moon appears to bob up & down in the sky**, serving as the "north star" from the 8th (when it first rises above the Rim Blizzard) to the 22nd (when it dips below the horizon). It does so because it orbits Aia at the same rate that Aia spins (a period of *precisely* 30 days), but at an incline of around 40°.

The Moon is *obviously* round because it catches light from the Sun in the same manner that a sphere in its place would; it has an extraordinarily unpredictable day/night rhythm as a result, but it is usually shrouded in dense clouds anyways, so it is hypothesized to be bleak & stormy.

Astronomers believe the Moon to be around 300 miles in diameter & situated at a distance of 5,000 to 8,000 miles from the center of Aia, or around 3,000 miles from the Telluric Veil.

## Practical Cosmology

A magician will usually know most of what's contained in this section.

The Universe is composed primarily of **elements** & **spirits**. There is also **metal** & the **gemstones**.

### Elements

There are 8 **elements**, of which 4 are *cardinal* or *oppositional* — a distinction that mostly only matters to alchemists, many of whom use alternative models of the elements.

| Element    | Additional Forms    | Opposition |
| ---------- | ------------------- | ---------- |
| Water      | Ice, clouds, acid   | Fire       |
| Fire       | Smoke, ash          | Water      |
| Stone      | Soil, quartz, clay  | Air        |
| Air        | Gas, lightning      | Stone      |
| Wood       | All plant tissue    | —          |
| Flesh      | Bone, chitin, blood | —          |
| Acid       | —                   | —          |
| Lightning  | —                   | —          |

A body of elemental matter is the source of Hit Dice (HD) in creatures.

### Spirits

A **spirit** is the soul possessing matter — most commonly Wood or Flesh, but occasionally the other elements as well. Spirits are immortal.

**Nothing is truly immaterial**. Even a ghost exists as a projection (an *illusion*) of a spirit from a corpse (& is *anchored* to that corpse). Even a human soul when extracted, though invisible to the normal man, resembles a small glimmering star (or a Will o' Wisp, p. @) through magician's eyes.

There is no such thing as a disincarnate god. Gods cannot appear anywhere they want or teleport around. **Every** god is a creature with a body. If they are worshiped, it is because they have built a cult as a religious Faction around themselves.

#### Divine Hierarchies

Gods are fractious creatures. Since they can't be everywhere at once, most of them tolerate local cults so long as that cult is *nominally* a part of an aligned pantheon. In practice, most local cults owe allegiance to & are Subfactions of the local lord or are *wholly* independent. On the other hand, many gods are also temporal rulers.

The boundary between a local lord & a local demigod is exceptionally thin.

#### Elemental Spirits

A stable expression of an element can be home to spirits, which are generally called *elementals*. These are not common & most elemental matter is devoid of a spirit.

### Metals & Gemstones

Metal is not elemental. There are 8 metals: Lead, tin, copper, iron, mercury, silver, gold, & bismuth. Similarly, there are an enormous variety of gemstones, the vast majority of which are associated with a handful of domains. None have special significance.

### Magnets & the Moon

The Heart of All Iron is said to be held by the Lunarian Princess on the Moon (p. @). Iron longs to return to it. This is why iron is magnetic & why it orients, in the absence of other magnetized iron, towards the Moon.

### Magician Demography

Most societies have approximately 1 magician per 100 people. At most, 5 of every 100 can practically be magicians — in the sense of possessing the temperament, intelligence, & skill required to do so.

### Elemental Hierarchy

Elementals have a saturated & mostly fixed hierarchy that rarely changes. A river is composed of droplets forming creeks forming branches forming a river. Each river is part of a much larger watershed. In theory, you could call upon the waters of an entire land at once. In practice, they would never answer you in unison.

The earth, air, acid, lightning, & a firestorm are similar.

### Divine Benefits Cancel Out

Just like how we don't explicitly model elemental hierarchies, we don't explicitly model the myriad pantheons of spirits & gods that exist across all human societies on the basis that it cancels out en masse. *Everyone* maintains a basic relationship to the gods that matter most to them — rice, water, war, threshold, ancestral spirits, so there's no need to give a +1 to anyone for explicitly stating that they're doing that.

We only model the effects of gods in the following cases:

- **Exceptional Devotion**: A person or group of people is exceptionally devoted to a single entity. Creates both benefits & vulnerabilities.
- **Exceptional Neglect**: You have really angered a *specific* god.
- **Competitive Advantages**: A person or group's access to a specific god or set of gods grants *unique* competitive advantages that others don't have.

### @ Settlement Gods

Worship is zero-sum. It costs resources & most people only have so much to give.

A god is constrained by their physical nature. Since a god is an actual creature with creature-like powers, they are often not worshiped outside of their center of activity.

Demigods are generated as monsters that occupy Locations on the map. There is generally 1 per 5-10 settlements?

Many demigods are humans that have acquired a lot of features over time.

### Making More Spirits

Making spirits requires you murder powerful creatures & imprison their souls.

### Why not just send ghosts to fight for you?

It's super expensive to send disembodied spirits to do anything. Almost impossible, actually; nothing can be truly immaterial & a spirit by itself is mostly helpless. A ghost is just a tortured spirit stuck in a damaged Totem (normally, their own corpse). You can embed a soul in a skeleton, but why? It'd usually be easier & less expensive to just send a regular living soldier.

## @ Apotheosis

A character with a cult & Godhead can treat their largest cult as the source of their Level even if they are not personally the Leader of that cult.

If they increase in Level by virtue of their cult's expansion, then rather than taking a standard Class Level, they can take a Level in a divine Class related to their Domain (p. @).

Divine Classes convey supernatural powers related to the field that the character is purported to specialize in. Whatever people are praying to you for is the thing that you're qualified for a divine Class in.

Besides the nature of the abilities unlocked, there is no special advantage to having Class Features from a divine Class.
