The Move: [[moves/move-hunting|Hunting]] covers the mechanical procedure for organized hunts. The rules below cover the procedure for individual or small-party wilderness hunting.

### Tracking

To track a creature, characters must find signs (footprints, scat, broken vegetation, droppings). The GM sets a difficulty based on the creature's size, the terrain, and how recently it passed:

- **Soft ground** (mud, snow, sand): Tracks are obvious. No Save needed.
- **Hard ground** (rock, dry earth): Requires a Save at -2 to find signs.
- **Rain or darkness**: Additional -2.

**Losing the trail**: If the trail ends (water crossing, rock shelf, nightfall), characters may search a 50' radius for the next sign. This takes 1 hour and requires a Save at -2.

### Stalking

Once the creature is located, characters must approach without being detected. The GM may call for a Save, modified by:

- **Distance**: +1 at long range (200'+), -1 inside 50'.
- **Cover**: +1 for thick forest or darkness.
- **Wind direction**: +1 if downwind.
- **Creature alertness**: -1 if the creature is prey, -2 if predator, -3 if supernatural.

If the stalk fails, the creature is aware of the party's presence. It may flee, hide, or attack.

### Harvesting

A successful hunt yields materials from the creature. The [[moves/move-hunting|Hunting Move]] provides the Faction-level structure for managing hunting expeditions, yields, and rare beast harvests.

Common yields include:

- **Meat**: 1d6 days of food per HD of the creature.
- **Hide/Pelt**: Value depends on quality and rarity. 1 sp to 50 sp.
- **Bones/Teeth/Claws**: Can be crafted into tools, weapons, or adornments.
- **Special organs**: Some creatures have alchemical or magical components.
- **Ritual cooking**: See the creature's bestiary entry for when_eaten effects.
