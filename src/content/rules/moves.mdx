A **Move** is any action taken in-game by an actor. Moves can be long or short-term actions; they may involve a chance of failure (& if they do, probably invoke a Save). They may involve movement, thinking, or long-term labor.

Moves may be **instant** or **extended**.

- An *instant* Move takes just a few seconds (& is called an Action).
- An *extended* Move has a Deadline set some period into the future, often days or months out; until that Deadline is reached, the character is dedicating all their time to continuing the Move & isn't able to perform other Moves.

Characters who lead Factions may oversee their Faction performing a Move instead of taking Moves personally.

<Callout type="gm" heading="What if we don't have Moves for what we want to do?">
You can think up & execute on actions for which there are no Moves. This is okay; it's the GM's job to determine reasonable outcomes in the absence of Moves.

If it turns out to be something that keeps coming up, the GM can also construct new Moves as necessary to meet the needs of the direction that play goes in.
</Callout>

## Actions

**Actions** are a special kind of Move lasting only a couple of seconds. They're mostly used within the context of Skirmish combats, but they can also be used outside of this to resolve similar actions in other contexts.

If a character ever has a "split second" to react to something, they have enough time to perform a single Minor Action.

Actions are further split into the Full-Turn, Major Action, Minor Action, and Free Action categories.
