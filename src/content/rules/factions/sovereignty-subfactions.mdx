Many of a Faction's Actions are delegated to **Subfactions**, which are **Subordinate** Factions. Each Subfaction is led by its own Leader, who is a **Retainer** of their **Liege** (the Leader of the master Subfaction). Factions with no master are **Sovereign**.

A Faction can be **Subordinate** to *at most* one Faction, and is also considered Subordinate to any Faction *above* their Liege if their Liege is not **Sovereign** (a Faction that is not Subordinate to any other Faction is Sovereign).

<Callout type="example">
An Earl whose direct Liege is a Count is *also* a Subordinate Subfaction to the *Count's* Liege, the King of England. The Earl cannot have a second Liege — for who can serve two masters without loving one & despising the other?
</Callout>
