Glass Empires uses exclusively six-sided dice. We call them d6 dice, where 6 indicates their number of sides. While other systems often use d4, d8, d10, or other polyhedral dice, we stick to six-sided dice for convenience & ease of play.

Besides this, this rulebook uses a couple of other conventions for expressing dice & other notation:

- **d66**: This means that you should roll two d6s and read them as tens and units (11–66, 36 results).
- **@ (stub marker)**: An @ symbol in the text marks a placeholder — content that is planned but not yet written. Ignore it during play; it will be replaced in a future update.
- **X-in-6**: Probability expressed as X chances in 6. A 3-in-6 chance is 50%. Roll 1d6; succeed if the result is X or lower.

## Reading Dice Expressions

| Expression | Meaning                                   |
| :-----------: | :------------------------------------------ |
|   **2d6**   | Roll two six-sided dice and sum           |
|  **1d6+2**  | Roll one d6 and add 2                     |
| **2d6−1** | Roll two d6s, sum, subtract 1             |
| **1d6×10** | Roll one d6 and multiply by 10            |
|   **d66**   | Roll two d6s as tens/units (11, 12 … 66) |
