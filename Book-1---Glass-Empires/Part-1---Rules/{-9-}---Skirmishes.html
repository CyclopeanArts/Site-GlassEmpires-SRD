{ 9 } Skirmishes

{ 9 } - Skirmishes

Mar 22, 202612 min read

One of the two potential playmodes for resolving combat. Skirmishes are intended for detailed or intricate fast-paced combat between a small number of relatively powerful individuals in a constrained combat area. They take longer to resolve than Battles and should be restrained to combats that hold a great deal of significance to the game.

Battles take place as a series of Rounds. Each Round represents a brief period of time (a few seconds) wherein each character receives a Turn on which they may perform Actions (extremely short-duration Moves) to eliminate their enemies or otherwise secure their objectives.

Skirmishes require the following components for a battle:

  • A map of the battlefield & its terrain;
  • Some means of measurement of distance — by default, a ruler;
  • Markers to indicate combatant locations.

Round Order

Rounds in Skirmishes have 3 phases.

  1. The GM provides the players a brief overview of the current situation, if necessary. He informs them of what the enemies appear to be planning on doing this Round; he may fully describe the intended Actions of slower NPCs, and not at all the Actions of faster NPCs at his discretion.
  2. All players take Turns in whichever order they figure (or simultaneously).
  3. All enemies take their Turns in whichever order the GM determines (or simultaneously).

Some characters may have Features that let them act before or after all other characters. In such a case, the Round phases look more like this:

  1. Overview provided
  2. Fast characters take Turns
  3. Ordinary characters take Turns
  4. Slow characters take Turns

In all cases, PCs move before NPCs. If for any reason two NPCs or PCs are fighting each other, assign odds to & roll a d6 to determine who goes first.

Surprise Rounds

A Surprise Round is a special kind of Round where only characters who are ambushing another set of combatants may act.

Turns & Actions

On their Turn, characters may:

  • Take 1 Major Action & 1 Minor Action.
    • A Major Action can be used to take a Minor Action, but not vice-versa.
  • Take as many Free Actions as desired.
  • Take a Full-Turn Action if they perform no Movement & take no other Actions on their Turn (excepting Free Actions).

On other character’s Turns, when other Actions occur (or at any time), characters may also take up to one Reaction per Round.

@ Movement & Space in a Skirmish

Glass Empires by default uses a gridless approach to combat.

Character Size

A character occupies a cylindrical battlefield area whose diameter & height is equal to their Size. If you’re using miniatures, you should try to make sure their bases are scaled appropriately to the battlefield scale in use.

Measuring Distance

A ruler is recommended! Any scale can be used for a given combat space so long as everyone’s using the same measurement system. The recommended scale is 1” of map space per 5ft in-game.

Character movement rate, when reduced or increased over a part of a journey, is measured from the point of impairment to the end of it.

Mixing Speed Scores

If a character uses multiple Speed scores for movement on their Turn, the lowest Speed score is the cap on how far the character can Move in total, overriding any higher scores used.

Compelled Movement

Compelled movement, such as falling, being shoved, or knockback is not counted against a character’s Speed score for determining movement rate or total moved distance during their Turn(s).

Difficult Terrain

Terrain or space that’s unusually difficult to navigate smoothly (such as stairs or thin suspended boards/walkways) halves a character’s movement rate.

Trampling

Actors may move through the space of foes whose Weight is less than one-tenth their own. If they are Dashing, Sprinting, or Charging, they deal 5 Physical Damage in the process to anyone in the way. If their Weight is less than one-hundredth their own, the Damage is increased to 50.

Skirmish Actions

Skirmish Actions are Moves that take a very brief amount of time to resolve. These Moves can be used outside of Skirmishes to resolve what can be accomplished with short-term action durations — generally speaking, a moment’s notice in reaction time is a single Major Action.

Attack (Major Action)

Declare a single target who you intend to harm & how (kill, shove, knock out). If the defender allows it, it happens. If not, perform 1 or more Strikes; by default, characters receive 1 Strike per Attack (but may receive more through Features or other abilities).

In order to attack a target, they must be within your weapon’s reach. A character’s melee reach is equal to their Size by default. Ranged attacks use the weapon’s listed range as its reach.

To perform a Strike against a chosen valid target, choose an available weapon & roll a Save. You may not add personal Fact bonuses to a Save, but should otherwise add factors such as the environment, Conditions, & the like.

Ranged Weapon Rules

The following extra rules apply only to ranged attacks.

  • Out of Range: Ranged attacks may be performed at up to twice a weapon’s listed range, but when doing so, each Strike Save suffers a −1 penalty.
  • Firing into Melee: When firing on a foe engaged in melee, the victim is chosen by random die roll from among all those visible within that foe’s melee reach whose Heights are greater than half the intended target’s Height.
  • Cover: If the target has low/weak cover (half a character’s Height), ranged attacks’ Strike Saves suffer a −2 penalty. If the target has high/strong cover (covering their whole body), it’s a −4 penalty.

The Angry Step

While starting an Attack Action & while not suffering any terrain or Condition that limits or alters your free movement, you may choose to move a distance up to or equal to your melee reach before making any Strikes. This extra distance is called the Angry Step & it does not count against your movement for your Turn.

Cast (Major Action)

Perform the Casting Move (p. @).

Dash (Full-Turn Action)

Move up to twice any of your Speed scores. You must Save to pass through any form of difficult or slowing terrain without falling.

Sprint (Full-Turn Action)

Move at least & up to 3× any of your Speed scores, turning no more than 45° throughout the entire distance. You must Save to pass through any form of difficult or slowing terrain without falling.

Charge (Full-Turn Action)

Move at least & up to 3× any of your Speed scores in a straight unobstructed line & then perform an Attack at the end as per the Attack Action’s rules. At the end of the Attack, the target, if less than half your Weight, must Save or Panic.

Interact (Minor Action)

Swap items around in your hands, sheathe or draw a weapon, pick up an item within your reach, or interact with something nearby (lever, door, etc). Light a torch, lantern, or flask.

Take Cover/Stand Up (Minor Action)

Become Prone (p. @) or recover from the Prone Condition.

Reload/Unjam (Major Action)

Reload or unjam a crossbow or windgun.

Set Down Gently (Major Action)

Set something down gently without damaging it — especially bags, backpacks, or fragile items.

Search Your Pack (Full-Turn Action)

Search a backpack or other bag for a specific item & pull it into your hand.

Jump (Minor Action)

Attempt a jump as per the rules for jumping (p. @).

Movement (Minor Action)

Move a distance up to one of your Speed scores (Run, Dig, Swim, Fly) across the appropriate medium. Keep in mind:

  • You may not take other Actions while in the middle of a Move; you must end your Move first.
  • Be mindful of Engagement (p. @) & Grappling (p. @), which punish & prevent normal movement respectively.
  • You may not move through space occupied by a foe or stop within space occupied by an ally.

Disengage (Free Action)

Break Engagement (p. @) with any number of enemies. If you don’t also move to a location outside their melee reach by the end of your Turn, you’ll be Engaged again.

Condition: Grappled

Grappling is a Condition that affects both characters. Neither character can move except by Shoving the other, which is also the only way to break a Grapple. Melee attacks gain a +2 to hit anyone in a Grapple, even if the attacker is outside the Grapple.

Characters who are more than twice the Weight of the largest opponent in a Grapple may freely move around, dragging the Grapple with them.

Extinguish Fire (Full-Turn Action)

Drop to the ground (becoming Prone) & simultaneously remove your own Burning Condition (p. @).

Engagement

When you enter an enemy’s reach, you become Engaged with them. Not as in marriage; rather, if you attempt to leave their reach without first taking a Disengage Action (p. @), they may perform a free Strike (p. @) on you with any valid weapon. Characters may perform any number of free Strikes in response to enemies leaving their reach without first disengaging.

Flight/Pursuit

Flight & pursuit is played out as a single Round of Battle (p. @) between all pursued & pursuers.

Panic & Fear

Characters Save to avoid Panic (synonymous with terror, fear, etc.) when any of the following happens:

  • Their Group is harmed by a Strike for the first time.
  • They personally reach ½ HP.
  • When charged with threat of trampling.
  • Whenever something spectacular, frightening, or sorcerous happens.
  • A hostile Spell is cast whose HD exceeds their HD.

Panicked NPCs flee from the source of their battle. Panicked PCs take doubled Damage (& other negative effects/durations, where applicable) from all sources.

Panic lasts until the end of combat. If Panic occurs outside of combat, it lasts for 1 hour; NPCs who are Panicked outside of combat will flee from it & otherwise be anxiously trying to avoid the source of their fear.

Mounted Combat

Mounts are combatants if they’re either sapient or carnivorous. Otherwise, they’re noncombatants. The following adjustments apply for mounts & their riders:

  • Saddles: Riders without saddles must Save each time the mount takes damage or fall from a distance equal to half the mount’s Size.
  • Incapacitation: If a mount or rider is Incapacitated (p. @) or killed, the rider(s) must Save or fall from their saddle(s). A non-combatant mount will flee battle if all riders are Incapacitated or killed.
  • Movement: Uses the mount’s scores.
  • Attacks: Non-combatant mounts can only attack with their rider during a Charge Action. Combatant mounts get their own set of Actions on their rider’s Turn.

Sapient or Panicked (p. @) mounts may ignore their riders’ wishes & take erratic action at the GM’s discretion.

Condition: Concentration

Concentration is a Condition for characters who are preoccupied with an ongoing activity & cannot focus clearly. Concentration ends if a character takes any amount of Damage or falls under a Condition that precludes Concentration (such as being Incapacitated). In addition, characters may have to Save up to once per round to maintain Concentration if exposed to violent motion (a moving mount, ship deck in a storm) or violent weather (blinding rain, hail).

If a character loses Concentration, whatever task they were focused on fails automatically.

Condition: Burning & Ignition

Characters who are ignited catch the Burning Condition. While Burning, at the start of each of their Turns, characters must Save or take [Power] Fire Damage.

The Burning Condition ends if a character is submerged in water. Characters may also receive a Save to attempt to put out a fire by spending a Full-Turn Action rolling on the ground.
